What are oscilloscopes?

Oscilloscopes (aka O-scopes) are a type of testing equipment used for testing electronics. They measure incoming voltages and graphically display them over time. This typically creates a waveform that people can analyze.

What are they used for?

Oscilloscopes are used to observe how electrical signals change. When connected to electronics such as a television or radio, they can display defects in the wiring.

Oscilloscopes don't necessarily have to work with electronics. Although they may be hooked up electronically, they can measure other types of signals as well. For example, cardiac monitors are technically a type of oscilloscope. This versitility makes oscillocopes useful for sound and audio as well.

In sound, oscilloscopes are used to observe sound waves. The image represents a digital. Over time, the program displays the waveform of the audio.

The Vectorscope

Some oscilloscopes have a feature which allows them to display two inputs at a time. In this mode, the first input becomes represented in the X axis, while the second corresponds to the Y axis. Using this mode, people can analyze the correlations between the two inputs.

Using a similar concept to the Etch A Sketch, people can create graphics on the oscilloscope. They can use the two inputs to rapidly draw certain shapes, essentially creating a vector monitor. These types of oscilloscopes are called vectorscopes.

In this image, there is vectorscope being used to display a 24-hour clock.

Tennis for Two

Tennis for Two was one of the first video games. The game was visually represented using a vectorscope. The ball would be electrically drawn as it bounced around the screen. It was controlled by two primitive controllers that had one knob and one button each.

Music Production

Vectorscopes, when tuned to the left and right channels of an audio signal, help music creators visuallize the space within their songs. Using this tool, people can observe what kind of presence certain sounds have in a song.

For instance, using a vectorscope can display how wide or mono a signal is. When the left and right channels are out of phase by a certain amount, they seem wide to the human brain. 

Oscilloscope Music

Even though people could draw visuals on the oscilloscope for a long time, the two inputs, when transformed into audio, did not sound very pleasant.

Jerobeam Fenderson, an electronic artist, sought to create media that both looks and sound pleasing when played on an oscilloscope. Consequently, he pioneered the genre of oscilloscope music.

In 2015, Jerobeam began a Kickstarter to gain funding for his hobby. Using this funding from supporters, he was able to create an album that provides a fascinating audio-visual experience.

"What you hear is what you see."

OsciStudio

Jerobeam's partner, Hansi, has created software that connects to Blender, a popular and free modelling app. Using this app (named OsciStudio), people have the ability create anything in a 3D space and display it on an oscilloscope.

The software also comes with a feature where people can change different parameters, such as size and rotation, and arrange different models in a timeline. This feature allows people to create long sequences of model animations.

Using a combination of OsciStudio and self-created sounds and patches, Jerobeam Fenderson creates songs that are unlike anything the world has ever seen (or heard).
